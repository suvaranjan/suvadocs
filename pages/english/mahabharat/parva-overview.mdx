## Parva Overview

The **Mahabharata** is a grand epic divided into 18 main parts called *parvas*. Each *parva* details different events and aspects of the epic. Here’s a summary of what happens in each part:

#### 1. Adi Parva (The Book of the Beginning)
The Adi Parva is the first book of the Mahabharata. It describes the birth and lineage of the main characters, including the Pandavas and Kauravas. It also details the origins of the kingdoms, the stories of sages, and the early events leading to the conflict. The famous incident of Draupadi's Swayamvara also occurs in this parva.

#### 2. Sabha Parva (The Book of the Assembly Hall)
This parva narrates the events of Yudhishthira's coronation, the Rajasuya Yajna, and the infamous dice game. Yudhishthira loses his kingdom, his brothers, and Draupadi in the dice game to the Kauravas, which leads to the Pandavas’ exile. Draupadi’s humiliation in the Kaurava court is a key event in this parva.

#### 3. Vana Parva (The Book of the Forest)
This parva covers the Pandavas' 13 years of exile in the forest. During this time, they endure hardships, gain knowledge from sages, and strengthen their resolve. The parva also includes many mythological tales and stories of the gods, as well as Bhima’s adventures and Arjuna’s quest for divine weapons.

#### 4. Virata Parva (The Book of Virata)
After their 12 years in exile, the Pandavas spend their final year living incognito in the kingdom of Virata. They serve in disguise in King Virata’s court and successfully remain hidden from the Kauravas. The parva ends with the Kauravas attacking Virata’s kingdom and the Pandavas revealing their identities.

#### 5. Udyoga Parva (The Book of the Effort)
This parva focuses on the efforts to negotiate peace between the Pandavas and Kauravas before the war. Lord Krishna acts as a peace messenger but fails to bring about a reconciliation. Preparations for the inevitable war begin, with both sides gathering their armies and allies.

#### 6. Bhishma Parva (The Book of Bhishma)
This parva describes the first part of the Kurukshetra War, with Bhishma leading the Kaurava army. The famous Bhagavad Gita, the conversation between Krishna and Arjuna, is revealed in this parva. Bhishma fights valiantly but is eventually felled by Arjuna, who shoots him with arrows under the guidance of Shikhandi.

#### 7. Drona Parva (The Book of Drona)
In this parva, Drona takes command of the Kaurava army after Bhishma’s fall. The parva covers the battles led by Drona, his strategic maneuvers, and his ultimate defeat. It also highlights the heroic deeds and significant events that occur during his command.

#### 8. Karna Parva (The Book of Karna)
This parva focuses on Karna, who becomes the commander of the Kaurava army after Drona’s death. It details Karna’s valor in battle, his confrontation with Arjuna, and his tragic fate. Karna’s struggle with his past and his loyalty to Duryodhana are central themes in this parva.

#### 9. Shalya Parva (The Book of Shalya)
Shalya takes over as the commander of the Kaurava forces following Karna’s death. This parva details the final stages of the Kurukshetra War, including Shalya’s strategies and the intense battles. It concludes with Shalya’s death and the ultimate downfall of the Kaurava army.

#### 10. Sauptika Parva (The Book of the Sleeping Warriors)
This parva recounts the night raid led by Ashwatthama against the Pandava camp. Ashwatthama kills many of the Pandava warriors while they are asleep, leading to a significant massacre. The parva emphasizes the brutal aspects of the war and the consequences of Ashwatthama’s actions.

#### 11. Stri Parva (The Book of the Women)
The Stri Parva deals with the aftermath of the war from the perspective of the women who have lost their loved ones. It explores the grief, sorrow, and reflections of the women of both the Kaurava and Pandava sides. The parva provides a poignant commentary on the human cost of war.

#### 12. Shanti Parva (The Book of Peace)
This parva focuses on the efforts to establish peace and order after the war. It contains the discourses of Bhishma on various subjects, including governance, duty, and morality. Shanti Parva aims to provide guidance on how to rebuild society and uphold righteousness.

#### 13. Anushasana Parva (The Book of the Instructions)
The Anushasana Parva continues Bhishma’s instructions on various topics, including law, ethics, and administration. It provides detailed guidelines for leading a virtuous life and administering a kingdom. The parva is a comprehensive manual on moral and ethical conduct.

#### 14. Ashvamedhika Parva (The Book of the Horse Sacrifice)
This parva describes the Ashvamedha Yajna performed by Yudhishthira to establish his authority and ensure the prosperity of his kingdom. It includes the details of the horse sacrifice ritual and the challenges faced during its execution.

#### 15. Ashramavasika Parva (The Book of the Hermitage)
The Ashramavasika Parva deals with the later years of the Pandavas' lives, focusing on their retreat to the forest and their lives in the hermitage. It explores themes of renunciation, spiritual reflection, and the final stages of their earthly existence.

#### 16. Mausala Parva (The Book of the Clubs)
This parva describes the destruction of the Yadava dynasty due to infighting and the cursed fate of Krishna’s clan. It portrays the internal conflicts and eventual collapse of the Yadavas, leading to the end of Krishna’s earthly incarnation.

#### 17. Mahaprasthanika Parva (The Book of the Great Journey)
The Mahaprasthanika Parva narrates the final journey of the Pandavas as they leave their kingdom to embark on a pilgrimage to the Himalayas. It describes their physical and spiritual journey towards the end of their lives and their quest for liberation.

#### 18. Svargarohana Parva (The Book of the Ascent to Heaven)
This parva covers the Pandavas' ascent to heaven in their mortal bodies. It concludes the epic by depicting their final moments and their ultimate attainment of heaven. The parva emphasizes the divine reward for their righteousness and sacrifices.

##### Khila - Harivamsa Parva (The Book of the Genealogy of Hari)
The Harivamsa is an addendum to the Mahabharata that covers the life and deeds of Lord Krishna, which are not detailed in the main 18 parvas. It includes the genealogical account of the Yadava dynasty and Krishna’s divine exploits and miracles.
